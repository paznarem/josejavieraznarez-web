Online psychological consulting and advisory services

Area of intervention

José Javier is a consulting psychologist specialised in contextual therapies and behavioural teaching and learning processes applied to dysfunctional everyday performance affected by stress, anxiety, sadness, restlessness, fear, low mood… across relational, family, social, professional, parenting, and specifically artistic contexts.

The services he offers as a psychologist and educator, as specified on the website www.josejavieraznarez.com, cover a broad area of intervention in the form of consulting and advisory work that in no way constitutes a clinical or healthcare intervention.

A large body of research has demonstrated the effectiveness of the contextual model, in which new behaviours are learned that produce deep and lasting changes in the person.

Goals of psychotherapy

Within the therapeutic alliance, José Javier, working from the contextual therapies paradigm (third generation), acts as a guide throughout each client's psychological process, focusing his efforts on the exploration, understanding, acceptance, validation, and change of behaviour from a place of freedom and creativity.

His contextual intervention is embedded in a behavioural teaching and learning process, focusing on the development of alternative behaviours to patterns of struggle, control, avoidance, and escape, with the primary goal of improving professional, social, and family functioning aligned with the life each client wishes to live.

In particular, José Javier addresses the mitigation and acceptance of self-criticism, guilt, insecurity, and shame, as well as the futile control over evaluative memories and persistent worries about the future that contribute to unease, restlessness, sadness, emotional tension, and anxiety in social performance.

Furthermore, he seeks to deactivate struggle and control responses against thoughts, memories, emotions, and sensations, thereby creating a space conducive to living life in accordance with goals and objectives that reflect genuine individual values. This process includes building a functional, contextual, and insightful self-evaluation, fostering a self-compassion-based perspective and decoupling self-worth from performance and external evaluation.

Specifically in the context of musical performance, José Javier works to enable each musician to approach auditions with present-moment awareness of their actions, with greater authenticity and emotional openness, thereby contributing to a more genuine and fulfilling artistic expression.

Confidentiality and data protection

All information shared during sessions will be treated with the strictest confidentiality, in compliance with Organic Law 3/2018, of 5 December, on the Protection of Personal Data and Guarantee of Digital Rights (LOPDGDD), and Regulation (EU) 2016/679, the General Data Protection Regulation (RGPD/GDPR).

All personal data requested and provided is incorporated into a privately held file whose sole controller and recipient is José Javier Aznárez Barrio. Only data strictly necessary to adequately provide the consulting and advisory service will be requested, and it will be retained for no longer than necessary for the purpose of the process or as required by applicable legal obligations.

All data collected is subject to a confidentiality commitment as a professional psychologist and educator, with the legally established security measures in place, and under no circumstances will it be shared with or processed by third parties, whether natural or legal persons, without the prior consent of the client, guardian, or legal representative, except where strictly necessary for the proper provision of the service or a legal obligation.

Certain exceptions to confidentiality exist, including: situations where a serious and imminent risk to you or to others is identified; legal obligations, such as reporting to authorities in the event of a court order; and information shared in professional supervision, where the client's identity is protected.

You may review the full legal notice and privacy policy on this website.

Client rights

  • Voluntary participation: your participation in these services is voluntary, and you may decide to discontinue at any time without negative consequences. These are not healthcare or clinical services under any circumstances.
  • Access to information: you have the right to receive clear and understandable information about the assessment and the plan for addressing the barriers and difficulties in your performance.
  • Consultation with other professionals: you may seek a second opinion from another professional if you consider it necessary.
  • Questions and comments: you may raise questions at any time and express any doubts or concerns about the services received.

Risks and benefits

Participation in contextual acceptance and change processes and behavioural teaching and learning processes may entail some risks:

  • Emotional distress: some interventions that implement previously avoided behaviours and responses may trigger difficult or uncomfortable emotions in the short term.
  • Changes in interpersonal relationships: misunderstandings and even tensions in relationships may initially arise due to changes in the client's behaviour, and the learning and implementation of new skills or personal perspectives aligned with their values.

There is evidence of benefits that include progressively living the life one wishes to live, changing what is open to change and accepting what has happened and what cannot be changed.
